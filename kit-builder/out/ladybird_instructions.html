7-spot Ladybird
Felt Puzzle

A Giant Insect World puzzle
build it · take it apart · build it again

You will need:

  • Red, black & white felt or paper
  • Scissors
  • This instruction sheet and template
  • The green felt mat for insect habitat (optional)

How to build it

  1. 1Trace each shape onto the right felt colour.
  2. 2Cut out all 17 pieces carefully.
  3. 3Lay the green mat down — that's your meadow!
  4. 4Place the red body, then the black head and antennae.
  5. 5Tuck 6 legs under — three each side.
  6. 6Dot on the 7 spots. Done!
⭐

Did you know?

  • The 7-spot ladybird is Britain's most common!
  • It eats hundreds of aphids — gardeners love it.
  • Bright red warns birds it tastes horrible!
  • It can ooze yucky yellow blood from its knees!
  • They hibernate in big groups over winter.
⭐

The pieces

Find these colours in felt or paper

Red · Black · White

Using the template, cut out:

Body ×1 — red
Head ×1 — black (2 white cheek dots)
Legs ×6 — black (pointy ends)
Antennae ×2 — black (round ends)
Spots ×7 — black (small circles)

That's 17 shapes in total!

Plus 2 white cheek dots (optional).

⭐

Finished!

Your ladybird is about 7 × 6 cm.

Take it apart and build it again!

Move the spots around — every ladybird can be different!
